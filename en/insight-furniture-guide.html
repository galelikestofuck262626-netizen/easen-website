PU Leather vs Fabric: Which Is Better for Restaurant Booth Seating?

A practical guide for restaurant owners, designers and hospitality buyers

Upholstery choice is one of the most consequential decisions in commercial restaurant furniture. The wrong material can mean high cleaning costs, early replacement and a worn-out look within two years. The right choice lasts five to eight years and looks better with age.

Why This Decision Matters More in Commercial Settings

A residential sofa might host a few hours of use per day. A restaurant booth seat can be occupied continuously from lunch through late dinner — enduring constant friction, spills, cleaning agents and body heat across hundreds of cycles per week.

Material that performs well at home often fails in commercial hospitality environments within 12–18 months. Understanding the practical differences helps buyers select the right material for their specific operation.

PU Leather: Strengths and Limitations

Strengths

  • ✓ Easy to wipe clean — spills removed in seconds
  • ✓ Resistant to liquid absorption
  • ✓ Consistent colour across large batches
  • ✓ Lower maintenance cost over time
  • ✓ More hygienic for food service environments

Limitations

  • ✗ Lower-grade PU can peel or crack within 2–3 years
  • ✗ Less breathable — can feel warm in summer
  • ✗ May look less premium than genuine leather or high-end fabric
  • ✗ Quality varies significantly — confirm grade before ordering

Buyer Note on PU Leather Quality

Commercial-grade PU uses a thicker backing, higher abrasion-resistant coating and UV-stabilised pigments. Ask for the abrasion rating (Martindale cycles) and coating thickness before ordering. Standard commercial seating should reach at least 50,000 Martindale cycles.

Fabric: Strengths and Limitations

Strengths

  • ✓ More breathable and comfortable in warm climates
  • ✓ Higher perceived quality in premium dining settings
  • ✓ Wide range of patterns and textures for design impact
  • ✓ High-grade commercial fabric (100,000+ Martindale) is highly durable

Limitations

  • ✗ Absorbs spills more readily — requires faster cleaning response
  • ✗ Stain treatment is essential for food service use
  • ✗ More complex to clean thoroughly
  • ✗ Higher ongoing maintenance effort

Our Recommendation by Venue Type

☕

Casual Cafés & All-Day Dining

PU leather is the practical choice. Spills are frequent, cleaning must be fast. Commercial-grade PU with a thick coating will perform well for 4–6 years in this environment.

🍽️

Mid-Range & Premium Restaurants

Both materials work — the choice depends on your aesthetic. Consider a combination: fabric booth backs with PU seat pads for practicality and visual warmth.

🏨

Hotel Lounges & Fine Dining

High-grade commercial fabric — velvet, boucle or performance weave — elevates perceived quality significantly. With proper treatment and regular cleaning, commercial fabric outperforms PU leather in premium hospitality environments.

🌿

Outdoor & Semi-Outdoor Spaces

For outdoor café terraces and covered garden areas, use outdoor-grade PU or solution-dyed acrylic fabric with UV and water resistance. Standard indoor materials will degrade rapidly outdoors.
